# DEST-004: Shenandoah National Park -- Luray, VA

## Summary

Shenandoah National Park protects 200,000 acres of the Blue Ridge Mountains just two hours from Richmond. Skyline Drive runs the length of the park along the ridge crest, offering overlooks into the Shenandoah Valley to the west and the Piedmont to the east. It is the closest national park to central Virginia and works as a day trip or weekend destination.

## What to Do

- **Skyline Drive**: The 105-mile scenic road has 75 overlooks. Allow 3-4 hours for a full drive without stops, or a full day with hikes.
- **Old Rag Mountain**: The most popular hike in Virginia. 9.2-mile circuit with a granite rock scramble near the summit. Strenuous; allow 6-8 hours. Day-use ticket required.
- **Dark Hollow Falls**: Easy 1.4-mile round-trip waterfall hike from Skyline Drive mile marker 50.7. Good for families.
- **Big Meadows**: Open meadow with lodge, campground, and visitor center. Deer are abundant at dusk. The lodge has a taproom with local beer.
- **Fall Foliage**: Peak color is typically mid-October at the highest elevations, late October at lower. The park webcams and NPS social media post updates.
- **Luray Caverns**: Privately operated cavern system just outside the park. Touristy but genuinely impressive formations. Allow 1-1.5 hours.

## Known Issues

- TripAdvisor (4.5), Google (4.7), and NPS survey (4.4) ratings show a wider spread than most destinations. The overall_rating of 4.53 is a fair average but users should understand the sources measure different things. This is a minor transparency issue, not a data error.
- Skyline Drive closures in winter are well-documented by NPS but not always mentioned in travel blog posts that recommend "year-round" visits.
