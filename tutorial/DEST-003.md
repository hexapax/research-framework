# DEST-003: Charleston Historic District -- Charleston, SC

## Summary

Charleston's Historic District is one of the best-preserved colonial-era city centers in the United States, combining 18th-century architecture, Southern cuisine, and coastal charm. Consistently voted a top US travel destination by major travel publications, it works as both a long weekend trip and a full week with nearby beaches and plantations.

## What to Do

- **Walking Tours**: The historic district is compact and walkable. Self-guided or guided tours cover architecture, history, and ghost stories.
- **Rainbow Row**: The famous pastel row houses on East Bay Street. Best photographed in morning light.
- **Fort Sumter**: Take the ferry from Liberty Square to the island where the Civil War began. Allow 2.5 hours for the full trip.
- **Dining**: Charleston is a food destination in its own right. Husk (Sean Brock's Southern cuisine), FIG (local farm-to-table), and Slightly North of Broad are standouts. Reservations essential for dinner.
- **Charleston City Market**: Open-air market with sweetgrass baskets (a Gullah tradition), local art, and food vendors.
- **Day Trips**: Folly Beach (20 min), Magnolia Plantation (20 min), Angel Oak Tree on Johns Island (30 min).
