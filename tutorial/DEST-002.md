# DEST-002: Canaan Valley Resort -- Davis, WV

## Summary

Canaan Valley is a high-altitude valley in the Allegheny Mountains of West Virginia, offering four-season outdoor recreation. The state-run resort provides affordable lodging as a base for skiing in winter and hiking in summer. The area around Davis and Thomas has a growing food and art scene, though it remains very rural.

## What to Do

- **Winter**: Ski or snowboard at the resort (47 trails, 1 terrain park). Cross-country skiing and snowshoeing at the state park.
- **Summer/Fall**: Hike Dolly Sods Wilderness for alpine-like landscapes. The Blackwater Falls trail is an easy 0.25-mile walk to a spectacular waterfall overlook.
- **Stargazing**: The valley is one of the darkest areas on the East Coast. Bring binoculars.
- **Mountain Biking**: Timberline Mountain has lift-served downhill trails in summer.
- **Town of Thomas**: Quirky small town with TipTop restaurant, Purple Fiddle music venue, and Stumptown coffee.

## Known Issues

- **Travel time is wrong.** The 3-hour estimate comes from a WV Tourism blog that likely used Washington DC as the starting point. Google Maps shows Richmond to Davis is 4 hours 35 minutes under normal conditions. Mountain roads can add time in winter weather.
- **Pricing not re-verified.** The resort changed management in late 2025. Current rates should be checked directly before booking.
- The "47 trails" count includes terrain park features and may overstate the actual skiing variety compared to larger resorts.
